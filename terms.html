Nature of the Service

What Arcane Tracer Is
Arcane Tracer is a financial network intelligence tool. It identifies statistical anomalies in public federal spending data and maps connections between entities using public corporate registry records. It is an analytical starting point, not a determination of fact, not a legal finding, and not a replacement for professional investigation.

Arcane Tracer aggregates and connects data from publicly available government databases including USASpending.gov, OpenCorporates, the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals list, SEC EDGAR, CourtListener, state business registries, and the IRS Exempt Organizations database. All data displayed is sourced from these official public records.

Arcane Tracer is not: a law enforcement tool, a credit reporting agency under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, a fraud determination authority, a replacement for licensed investigators or attorneys, or a journalistic fact-checking service. Our Methodology page explains in full how risk scores are calculated, what flags mean, and what they explicitly do not mean.

The Most Important Thing to Understand
A risk flag or elevated score in Arcane Tracer does not mean an entity has committed fraud or any wrongdoing. The patterns we identify have innocent explanations in many cases. Our output is appropriate as a starting point for investigation. It is never appropriate as a sole basis for publishing an accusation, taking adverse action, or making a legal determination.

Data Sources and Accuracy

All data in Arcane Tracer is sourced from official public government databases. We do not create, estimate, or fabricate data. Every data point displayed includes the date it was retrieved from its source database.

We make no representation that the underlying data sources are complete, current, or free from error. Known limitations include reporting lags of 30 to 90 days on some federal payment programs, variation in state business registry update frequency, and potential delays in OFAC list updates. The full list of sources and their known limitations is published on our Methodology page.

Data freshness is displayed on every investigation output. Data points older than 90 days display a staleness warning. You are responsible for verifying any data point against the primary source before relying on it for any decision.

Verify Before Acting
Always verify Arcane Tracer findings against the original primary source records before taking any action. Links to original source records are provided in every investigation output precisely for this purpose.

Permitted Use of Output

Arcane Tracer output, including risk scores, flag descriptions, network graphs, AI intelligence briefs, and all related data, may be used for the following purposes when accompanied by independent verification and appropriate professional judgment:

  • Opening an investigation. Using output as a starting point to determine whether further investigation is warranted.

  • FOIA requests and public records research. Using identified connections to guide requests for additional public records.
