01

Our commitment: what this tool is and is not

The Core Principle
Every flag Arcane Tracer generates is a pattern observation derived entirely from public records. The existence of a pattern is not evidence of fraud. Patterns create investigative leads. Investigators follow leads. Courts determine facts. Arcane Tracer is a starting point, not an endpoint.

Arcane Tracer monitors public federal spending data and surfaces statistical anomalies: patterns that, based on documented fraud cases, warrant a closer look. It is a pattern detection engine, not a fraud determination system.

We do not determine guilt. We do not confirm wrongdoing. We are not a law enforcement tool, a credit reporting agency, or a replacement for legal due diligence. We aggregate and connect publicly available records and present the patterns we find with full source citations and explicit uncertainty about what those patterns mean.

What Arcane Tracer output is appropriate for: opening an investigation, requesting records under FOIA, conducting additional due diligence, or triaging a large dataset of entities. It is not appropriate as a sole basis for publishing an accusation, freezing an account, denying a contract, or any other action that materially harms a business or individual without independent verification.

The Stakes
A business wrongly flagged as high-risk may lose contracts, face regulatory scrutiny, or be damaged in the press. The burden of accuracy is on us. We treat false positives as serious failures, not acceptable edge cases. Every design decision in this system was made with this responsibility in mind.
02

Where the data comes from

Every data point Arcane Tracer uses comes from official public government databases. We do not scrape private sites, buy commercial data lists, or accept user-submitted information as input to risk scoring. If we cannot source a claim to a specific public record, it does not appear in our output.

Live
USASpending.gov
All federal contracts, grants, loans, and direct payments. The primary source for the anomaly detection feed.
Known limitation: some programs have a 30 to 90 day reporting lag on new awards.
Live
OpenCorporates
200M+ company records across 140 jurisdictions. Incorporation dates, registered agents, officers, addresses, and status.
Known limitation: not all states update in real time. Registry data can lag by weeks in some jurisdictions.
Live
OFAC SDN List
US Treasury's Specially Designated Nationals list. Every entity in every network is cross-referenced daily.
Known limitation: covers US-designated sanctions only. Does not include EU or UN sanctions lists.
Live
SEC EDGAR
All SEC filings for public companies. Beneficial ownership disclosures and financial statements.
Known limitation: only covers SEC-registered entities. Private companies are not included.
Live
CourtListener
Millions of federal court records. Civil suits, bankruptcies, and criminal cases searchable by entity name.
Known limitation: not all federal courts are current. Some records are sealed or delayed.
Live
State Business Registries
Corporate status, dissolution dates, and officer changes from all 50 states.
Known limitation: quality and timeliness of data varies significantly by state.
Live
IRS Exempt Organizations
Nonprofit status, revocations, and annual filings. Critical for identifying organizations that lost tax-exempt status.
Known limitation: revocations may not appear in the database for 30 to 60 days after they occur.
Coming Soon
FEC Campaign Finance
Federal campaign contribution records for mapping political connections in financial networks.
In development. Not yet active in the scoring engine.
Data Freshness Requirement
Every data point displayed in Arcane Tracer shows the date it was retrieved. We never present data as current without showing its source date. If a data point is more than 90 days old it displays a staleness warning. No risk score is presented without showing the dates of the underlying data it was built from.
03

How risk scores work

Every entity Arcane Tracer investigates receives a risk score between 0 and 100. The score is additive. Each confirmed flag adds a defined number of points. Scores are never generated by a black-box machine learning model. Every point in every score is traceable to a specific, verifiable data observation.

Score Label Visible in Public Feed
0 to 24 Unscored: insufficient signal to assess risk Never surfaced
25 to 44 Watch: one or more minor flags present Internal only
45 to 64 Elevated: multiple flags confirmed Yes, with full context and sources
65 to 79 High Risk: strong multi-flag pattern Yes, with full context and sources
80 to 100 Critical: severe multi-flag cluster Yes, priority surfaced
The Minimum Threshold Rule
An entity will not appear in the public anomaly feed unless it scores 45 or above AND has at least two independently confirmed flags from different categories. A single flag, no matter how severe, is never sufficient to surface an entity publicly. This rule is absolute.

Flags are not OR conditions. They are AND conditions. Every single required condition within a flag must be independently verified from a primary data source before that flag contributes any points to a score. A flag that partially matches does not fire. Ambiguous matches are noted separately and never counted toward a score.

04

The eight risk flags explained

Each flag in Arcane Tracer requires multiple independently verified conditions to fire. Below is a plain-English explanation of each flag, what it means, and, critically, what innocent explanations exist for each pattern. We publish these because understanding what a flag does not mean is as important as understanding what it does.

High Risk
New Entity Receiving Large Payment
Requires: age under 6 months + payment over $250K + zero prior contracts. All three required.
Legitimate contractors typically have years of operating history before receiving large federal payments. This flag fires only when all three conditions align. Innocent explanations include: legitimate startup that won a competitive bid, subsidiary of an established company, program specifically designed for new entrants.
High Risk
Registered Agent Manages High Volume of Same-Program Entities
Requires: 200+ entities + 30% in same program + 50% incorporated in same 90-day window
Mass-creation of entities for the same program by a single operator is a documented fraud pattern. This flag never fires against known national registered agent services such as CT Corporation or Northwest Registered Agent. Innocent explanations include: legitimate national service managing diverse clients, industry-specific agent specializing in a regulated sector.
High Risk
Multiple Entities at One Address Receiving Same Program Payments
Requires: 3 or more entities + same program + non-commercial address + 2+ incorporated within 90 days
Known commercial office buildings, coworking spaces, law firms, and registered agent service addresses are excluded entirely from this flag. It only fires at non-commercial addresses. Innocent explanations include: community organization hub, legitimate multi-program nonprofit, shared services arrangement.
Critical
Dissolved Entity Still Receiving Payments
Requires: confirmed dissolution date from state registry + payment received after that date
This is a binary factual flag. Either the state says the company is dissolved and a payment arrived after that date, or it did not. We account for registry data lag and display the source date of the dissolution record prominently. Innocent explanations include: entity reinstated after dissolution, registry data lag creating apparent overlap, IRS revocation which is different from state dissolution.
High Risk
Connected to OFAC-Listed Entities
Requires: exact officer name or registered agent match + OFAC-listed entity currently on SDN list
This flag specifically does not mean the entity itself is sanctioned. It means a connection exists through shared attributes. Officer name matches require exact first and last name in the same state and time period. Partial name matches do not qualify. Innocent explanations include: common name coincidence, former employee who later appeared on a sanctions list, legitimate shared service provider.
High Risk
Coordinated Incorporation Window
Requires: 3+ entities + shared attribute + same program + incorporation timed to program opening
Legitimate businesses form independently over time. When multiple entities sharing key attributes all form within weeks of a program opening, timing correlation becomes significant. Innocent explanations include: documented corporate restructuring, legitimate business consortium formation, industry association that assisted multiple members in forming entities.
Supplementary
No Prior Federal Contract History
Requires: zero USASpending history + entity over 12 months old + payment over $500K. Another flag must already be present
This flag never surfaces an entity on its own. It is a corroborating signal only, adding context when other flags have already fired. Every organization has a first contract; this flag simply notes when there is no track record to evaluate. Innocent explanations include: legitimate first-time contractor, organization that previously operated privately or with state funding only.
Elevated
Sudden Large Payment Spike
Requires: 5x increase + no contract modification on file + absolute increase over $1M + entity under 5 years history
This flag does not fire against established contractors with long histories, and it excludes periods where the program itself received a budget increase. Innocent explanations include: legitimate competitive contract win, program expansion award, emergency procurement situation, merger or acquisition bringing new revenue.
05

What we never flag

These categories are permanently excluded from risk scoring. No combination of flags changes this. If any proposed flag, score, or AI output references any of these categories as a basis for risk, it is removed before reaching any user.

  • Industry or sector. Being in healthcare, childcare, housing, or any other sector is not a risk signal. Sector-level fraud statistics describe populations, not individual entities.
  • Payment amount alone. Receiving a large federal payment is not inherently suspicious. Amount is only relevant as a threshold condition within a multi-condition flag, never as a standalone signal.
  • Geography. Operating in a city, state, or zip code with documented fraud history is not a risk signal for any individual entity in that area.
  • Demographic ownership. Minority-owned, women-owned, veteran-owned, or any other ownership characteristic is never used as a risk input, modifier, or flag under any circumstances.
  • Business age alone. Being a new business is not fraud. Age only contributes to a flag when combined with payment size, contract history, and agent patterns, never alone.
  • Prior news coverage or allegations. Media reporting, social media allegations, or prior public accusations about an entity are never used as scoring inputs. We score only verifiable public data records.
  • Political affiliations. Political donations, lobbying activity, or connections to political figures are not used as risk signals.
  • Foreign-sounding names. Entity names that appear foreign or non-English are never treated as risk signals. Name origin, language, or ethnic association is completely excluded from all scoring.
  • Single data point. Any situation where only one data source confirms a concerning pattern. One source is never enough. Independent corroboration from a second primary source is required for every flag.
06

Language standards: what every output must say

The language in every AI intelligence brief is governed by strict rules. These are enforced in the system prompt and validated before any brief is shown to a user. The distinction between observation and accusation is not semantic. It is the legal and ethical line we do not cross.

Required Phrasing Prohibited Phrasing
This pattern is consistent with...This entity is engaged in...
Investigators have noted that entities with this profile often...This entity has committed...
The data shows that [X] occurred on [DATE] per [SOURCE]The company fraudulently...
This warrants further investigationThis proves that...
Public records indicate...It is clear that...
One possible explanation for this pattern is...The only explanation for this is...
These flags are analytical observations onlyThese flags are evidence of...
This analysis is based entirely on public data and is not a legal conclusionFraud has been detected

Every AI brief must include a section explicitly stating the innocent explanations for the patterns found. This section is mandatory and cannot be omitted regardless of how strong the pattern appears. The full disclaimer appears at the end of every brief, every investigation view, and every shared link.

07

Disputing a flag

If your organization appears in Arcane Tracer and you believe a flag is inaccurate, you have the right to request a review. We take disputes seriously. A confirmed false positive is a failure of our system and we want to know about it.

Send a dispute request to support@arcanetracer.com with the entity name, the specific flag you are disputing, and any documentation that supports your position. We aim to respond to disputes in a reasonable timeframe. If a flag is confirmed to be in error, we work to remove it promptly and update our methodology to reduce the chance of the same error recurring.

What We Can and Cannot Change
We can correct flags that are based on inaccurate or outdated data. We cannot remove a flag solely because an entity disagrees with the pattern observation. If the data supports the flag under our methodology, it remains. As a general practice we aim to show you the exact data and source behind any flag on request.
