Privacy Policy

What data Arcane Tracer collects from you, how we use it, where we store it, and how to exercise your rights. We collect the minimum necessary to operate the service and we never sell your personal data.

01

Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Arcane Tracer, operated by Flavio DeOliveira LLC, collects, uses, stores, and shares personal data from people who visit arcanetracer.com, request waitlist or Professional access, or communicate with us by email.

This policy applies only to personal data about users of the service. It does not apply to information about organizations and entities that appear inside Arcane Tracer investigations, which is sourced entirely from public government records and is governed by our Terms of Use, Section 05.

02

Data We Collect From You

We collect the minimum data necessary to operate the waitlist, deliver Professional access, and maintain compliance with our Terms of Use. The categories of personal data we collect are listed below.

Waitlist and Access Request Data
  • Name. Provided by you during waitlist or Professional access registration.
  • Email address. Used to confirm your request and deliver access credentials.
  • Organization. The company or institution you represent, used to prioritize access tiers.
  • Professional use case. A short description of how you intend to use Arcane Tracer, used to evaluate fit and deliver appropriate access.
  • Terms acceptance record. A timestamp and terms version recorded at the moment you accept the Terms of Use, along with a basic device fingerprint used only to associate the acceptance with the request.
Payment Data (Professional Tier Only)

When you subscribe to Professional access, payment information is collected and processed directly by Stripe. Arcane Tracer does not see, store, or transmit your full payment card details. We receive only a subscription identifier, billing status, and the last four digits of the card used for reference.

Technical Data

When you visit the site we collect basic technical data including IP address, browser user agent, referrer, and pages visited. This data is used to operate the service, enforce rate limits on the free tier, and detect abuse. We do not use third-party advertising trackers.

What We Do Not Collect
We do not collect sensitive personal data such as government identification numbers, financial account numbers, health information, or biometric data. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16. We do not sell personal data to anyone under any circumstances.
03

How We Use Your Data

We use the personal data we collect only for the following purposes:

  • Delivering access. Sending waitlist confirmations, access credentials, and account information to the email address you provide.
  • Recording terms acceptance. Associating your accepted version of the Terms of Use with your access request for compliance purposes.
  • Operating the service. Authenticating Professional users, processing payments through Stripe, and enforcing rate limits on free tier access.
  • Product communications. Sending service-related updates, changes to terms, security notices, and occasional product news. You can opt out of non-essential communications at any time.
  • Fraud and abuse prevention. Using technical data to detect, investigate, and prevent abuse of the service or violations of the Terms of Use.

We do not use your personal data for behavioral advertising, profiling for marketing, or any purpose other than operating Arcane Tracer as described above.

04

How We Share Your Data

We share personal data only with the minimum set of vendors required to operate the service. Each of these vendors is contractually bound to process data only on our behalf and only for the specific purposes described here.

  • Supabase. Hosts our primary application database, including waitlist and Professional access records. Data is stored in encrypted form.
  • Stripe. Processes all Professional tier payments. Stripe is a PCI DSS compliant payment processor. Stripe maintains its own privacy policy which applies to payment data.
  • Resend. Delivers transactional email including waitlist confirmations and access credentials.
  • Vercel. Hosts the public website and handles incoming web traffic.
  • Railway. Hosts internal backend services used for access request processing.

We do not sell personal data. We do not share personal data with advertising networks, data brokers, or any party not listed above. We may disclose data in response to a valid legal process only to the extent required by law. Where legally permitted we aim to notify affected users, but we make no commitment to do so.

05

Information About Flagged Entities

Information about organizations, contractors, and other entities that appear inside Arcane Tracer investigations is sourced entirely from publicly available government records and databases. This information is not personal data collected from users of the service, and it is not governed by this Privacy Policy.

If you represent an entity that appears in Arcane Tracer and you believe a flag or score is based on inaccurate underlying data, our dispute process is described in Terms of Use, Section 05. Send disputes to support@arcanetracer.com.

06

Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary to operate the service and meet legal or compliance obligations.

  • Waitlist records. Retained until access is granted or for up to 24 months from submission, whichever comes first, after which the record is deleted.
  • Professional account records. Retained for the duration of the Professional subscription, plus 24 months after cancellation for billing and compliance purposes.
  • Terms acceptance records. Retained for the lifetime of the associated account, plus 24 months, to demonstrate compliance with our Terms of Use.
  • Technical logs. Retained for up to 90 days, then aggregated or deleted.

You may request deletion of your personal data at any time by contacting us at the address listed in Section 10. We aim to honor deletion requests in a reasonable timeframe except where retention is required by applicable law.

07

Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access. Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction. Request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Deletion. Request that we delete your personal data.
  • Objection. Object to our processing of your personal data for specific purposes.
  • Portability. Receive a copy of your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Withdrawal of consent. Withdraw consent to processing at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, email support@arcanetracer.com. We aim to respond in a reasonable timeframe and may ask you to confirm the email address on file before we act on a request.

08

Security

Arcane Tracer is operated by a single person and does not maintain a formal security audit program, SOC 2 certification, or a dedicated security staff. We rely on a small set of well known platform vendors to provide the underlying security posture for how your data is handled. User account passwords, when applicable, are stored as salted hashes rather than in plain text. Application data is hosted on Supabase, which provides the production database and manages encryption of data at rest. The public website is served through Vercel, and backend services run on Railway. Traffic to and from these platforms is encrypted in transit using the HTTPS and TLS connections those vendors provide. Payment details are handled directly by Stripe and are not stored on any system we operate.

No system is perfectly secure, and this one is no exception. In the event of a data breach that affects your personal data, we aim to notify you and any relevant regulators to the extent required by applicable law.

09

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, our vendors, or applicable law. When we make material changes we aim to notify Professional users by email in advance of the effective date. The effective date at the top of this page reflects the current version.
